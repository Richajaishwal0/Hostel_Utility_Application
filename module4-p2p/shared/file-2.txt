Crop diseases may infect various plant components; hence, they reduce both the amount and quality of yields. Early identification of diseases within plant parts prevents their spread(1). Series research for plant disease identification utilizing deep learning models has yielded data sets for rice, wheat, and corn to evaluate eight models. One model based on CNN was accurate for all crops. Early identification is vital for promoting food security and reducing crop losses(2).

A variety of works emphasize the importance of accurate and timely detection of diseases in image processing for improving agriculture. It covers various machine learning and deep learning methods, including CNNs, for efficient image-based detection, hence giving scope for further research in this area. Preprocessing techniques, like image enhancement and hybrid methods of CNNs, are emphasized so that better classification of diseases can be done for enabling farmers to take precautions for improving agricultural production. In a survey of different approaches of ML techniques for crop disease detection, it mentions the usage of SVM and Decision Trees to enhance crop productivity and ensure food security.A DenseNet169 MLP achieved an accuracy of 97.68% in crop disease detection and can help farmers in earlier diagnosis and increased productivity using deep learning techniques. There is some literature that suggests CNN and Bayesian learning techniques for reliable and earlier crop disease detection.

There was an effort to categorize the diseases in paddy plants; however, the paper was withdrawn due to incorrect claims and image problems. The discussions on deep learning for reviewing rice leaf disease detection emphasize automation, challenges, and the future direction, indicating the need for a hybrid approach in identifying diseases such as rice blast and bacterial blight.The combination of CNNs and simulated thermal imaging provides effective detection for paddy plant diseases and overcomes the problems associated with traditional techniques by considering temperature changes.

A CNN-based model was able to achieve an accuracy of 95% in rice disease identification, thus providing long-term solutions to agricultural losses and sustainable farming. Another review identified the use of innovative technologies for vegetable and fruit disease identification, discussing the limitations of the used data and efficiency these technologies are bound to provide to agricultural practices. Deep learning for the classification of plant diseases uses CNN, addressing the challenges developed in the data. Deep transfer learning techniques have produced an accuracy of 98.7% for paddy blast disease detection, hence ensuring sustainable and effective farming.Transfer learning using pre-trained CNN and SVM models in rice diseases made it possible to detect rice diseases with high accuracy, thus promoting rice health and productivity and opening venues to viable agricultural practices. The application of VGG16 in paddy leaf disease classification using transfer learning emphasized feature extraction and presented an effective level of accuracy with small dataset sizes to ensure effective agricultural practices.

A convolutional neural network based on multiscale feature extraction, image segmentation, and ensemble techniques achieved an accuracy of 99.75% in identifying wheat crop diseases, and Xception achieved the best results. The efficiency of transfer learning in identifying plant diseases using less data is emphasized in order to address practical issues and improve accuracy to promote sustainable agriculture through earlyAI-based systems.One-shot learning proves to be highly efficient when dealing with minimal data in the classification of plant diseases through Siamese networks and prototypical networks to enhance accuracy in precision agriculture and early crop management. Analysis of CNN architectures focuses on handling unbalanced datasets and different environmental conditions to enhance scalability through AI in precision agriculture, early diagnostics, and enhanced crop management.

A real-time monitoring system based on CNNs for the detection of leaf diseases is mentioned, which people consider a method for enhanced crop health, reducing errors, and providing cheaper agricultural solutions. The CNN-based model for diagnosing plant diseases from the leaves of rice and potato crops performs quite accurately; this proves the effectiveness of deep learning ideas for better agricultural growth.

The features of the ResNet model are highlighted by 94% accuracy in identifying diseases related to tomatoes and potatoes, as it enables farmers to reduce losses while increasing production. The classification of rice leaf diseases using machine learning approaches attracts attention related to traditional constraints and the ability of early detection and improved food security in agriculture.
